All Your Baseline

Baseline is traditionally defined as the invisible line where all lower case letters rest. The space between these lines and the caps of the characters underneath is referred to as leading which is literally based on strips of lead that would go between lines of text to space them out, this measurement is critical in establishing consistent vertical movement.

However, the idea of a baseline isn't 100% accurate when we switch contexts from the printed page to the browser. Instead of defining leading in css we use the property line-height. Visually, these two ideas are the same, but technically line-height is doing things a bit differently than leading. Instead of adding space to the bottom of each line, it adds a 'half-leading' to the top and bottom of each line vertically centering it between baselines.
